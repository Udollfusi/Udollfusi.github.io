Brian D. Winters

From 2000 to 2003, I was a Ph.D. student with Mark Greenstreet, at UBC in the ISD lab. My thesis research focused on utilizing the analog behavior of ostensibly digital circuits to improve performance. We looked at the event dynamics of circuits in order to gain qualitative and quantitative insights into their behavior. The research is ongoing, although I've moved on.

I completed my M.Sc. with Alan Hu in 2000, also in the ISD lab. My thesis describes an attempt to make an existing model checking tool more usable for novices.
